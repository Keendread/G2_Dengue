Overview of the Research

Background of the Study


Dengue

is a mosquito-borne viral infection causing flu-like symptoms, such as high fever, headache, muscle and joint pain, and rash. It remains as a pertinent issue in the health of people nationwide.

Fatality

In a report from GMA, since January to May this year, over 110,000 cases of dengue have been recorded by the Department of Health (DOH) resulting in 437 deaths. This is a 75% increase from last year and a 224% increase in Metro Manila.

Implemented Solutions

DOH have continued to work on campaigns such as "Alas Kwatro Kontra Mosquito", promoting mosquito control action such as through ‘Taob, Taktak, Tuyo, at Takip’ as well as providing dengue related hospital services.


Research Questions

Our research focuses on understanding dengue trends, and effects of government budgeting to waste management and other factors.

  1. What are the trends over time to the occurrences and severity of dengue cases?
  2. What regions face the greatest risk?
  3. Do geographical features of these regions correlate to risk?
  4. Did dengue cases decline in the COVID-19 pandemic?

Research Hypothesis

Based on our research questions, we propose the following hypotheses:

  • Null Hypothesis: There is no trend in the increase of Dengue over the past years.
  • Alternative Hypothesis: There is a significantly decreasing / increasing trend in the number of dengue cases over the past years.

Nutshell Plot

The Nutshell Plot shows data on new cases reported from DOH Surveillance Data between January, 2021 and September, 2025. The regression line shows an increase which coincides with the result of the Mann-Kendall Test performed on the data.

Project Plot 1

This choropleth plot helps us determine which regions the Philippines have more dengue cases per 100,000 population in that region. Based on this plot, the CORDILLERA ADMINISTRATIVE REGION have the most dengue cases among the rest of the regions.

Project Plot 2

This line graph with regression line shows the difference in cases during the Pandemic. The results show that during the Pandemic, there was a drop in new cases per month.


Hypothesis Testing

Null and Alternative Hypothesis Testing

The Mann-Kendall test was used to evaluate the hypotheses to detect monotonic trends in the dataset. The results of testing suggest an increasing trend in the number of Dengue cases from 2021-2025. Thus, the Alternative Hypothesis is accepted.
